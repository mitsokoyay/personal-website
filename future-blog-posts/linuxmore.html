Linux: the tool so nerdy, even other computer scientists call me a geek for using it. Linux and I have been acquainted for years- but it's only recently that I fell in love. Thinking back to 2017- I was in 5th grade, and only starting to get into this whole computer thing. Linus Tech Tips became my babysitter and there was nothing that I wanted more in the world than a fancy new Windows desktop. The GTX Titan Xp was the creme de la creme of graphics cards, it seeming like the unobtainium of the day. I fantasized about all the games I could play with a PC. I thought Windows was the obvious choice, and through helping my grandparents navigate their digital life, I already had a decent understanding of how it worked. But I kept hearing this name be thrown around- "Linux", usually made as either a passing sneer or with post-ironic praising my eleven year old mind didn't quite understand. All I knew was that it was free, and to me that meant it couldn't possibly hold a candle to Windows- I didn't quite understand the concept of open source just yet. With that, I wrote it off. Only a couple years later, I found myself with that fancy new Windows desktop. I was having no problems with Windows, but I saw a video on YouTube that piqued my interest. It was titled: "9 second boot time arch linux". Nine seconds? Is that even possible? Even my brand new gaming desktop takes 20+ seconds to boot.
